Operation Sawmill


Dr. Arthur O'Leary recognized the financial potential of the timber stands around Pine Creek. He began buying up large tracts of forest and made plans to build a sawmill.

Charles Wright would become the manager of the lumber operation at Pine Creek. In 1865, Daniel Thomas and William Woodward built a dock on John Major's property on Lake Michigan The sawmill was built on the south side of Pine Creek not far from Lake Michigan. It was a 2-story wood building typical of sawmills of that time. The creek was dammed to create a mill pond right in front of the sawmill.

Mill operation began in 1869 for the purpose of producing white pine lumber, and the town of Majorville was established.
